After graduating I did some traveling completing a Seattle to Cranston bicycle trip with a fellow Cranston West friend (Dave Fern). After dabbling in a few fields(I never went to college) I ended up on the West Coast(SF Bay Area) in the cable TV industry. After two years I decided to relocate back to the east coast and joined a commercial restaurant equipment company in Rhode Island that included a month's stay in Saudi Arabia (highly unrecommended). My travels then took me to Hawaii and the island of Maui. A paradise to be sure for vacationers. A challenge for anyone who decides to live there (such as myself). I was a caretaker at a condo resort and thoroughly enjoyed the walks to work along the beach. I then traveled back to Rhode Island and became heavily involved in soccer refereeing. After admitting to myself I could not make a living traveling all over New England blowing a whistle (although I loved it) I decided to relocate back to California in 1990. While there I stumbled upon the Southern Pacific Railroad. I then entered the strange brotherhood of Railroad Signalmen. My career progressed to where I am now designing railroad signal systems for General Electric Transportation in Omaha Nebraska. I have remained single during this entire odyssey..................quite a feat! I am thoroughly looking forward to my next adventure . My hobbies include physical fitness, wines, bicycling and of course my mother's Italian cooking(she still lives in Cranston). Best wishes to you and all our classmates from the great class of ' 71. As I write this I am listening to some music from the 60's and 70's..............probably the best music ever. Classic music and the class of ' 71 go together very well.

Ciao and Happy New Year.

Tony Romano
Omaha, NE.
