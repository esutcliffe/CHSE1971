After high school, I had a hard time finding a job, so I joined the Air Force. Trained in Texas. Mike Jones dropped by to visit while on a cross-country motorcycle trip. He was nearly killed as he left when a car hit him from behind doing 80 mph. He recovered and I was stationed in Maine
for a couple of years generating electricity for a radar site in Buck's Harbor, right on the Canadian border. Stationed in Thailand for a year after that. This gave me a completely different perspective on the world and on life.

After the USAF I returned to RI and lived in Jamestown for a couple of years. Worked as a foreign car mechanic (Scott Motors) and also as an electrician (Peco Electric.) The blizzard of '78 was the last straw. A lady friend, Mary Woods, had moved to Colorado, and invited me out. Packed up my 1970 beat-to-crap Ford Galaxy and headed West. When I see the ending of the movie "Good Will Hunting" a tear comes to my eye. My car looked exactly like that and I had a similar attitude. I didn't have enough money for the gas to come back, so I had no choice but to stay in Denver. Landed a job as an electrician. Stayed in the Belview Hotel. $22 a week, in advance. Step over the wino to get in the front door. Lived on water, peanut butter, and bread for two weeks until my first paycheck arrived.

Completely unbeknown to me, Mike Jones had moved to Denver (from California) at the same time I moved to Denver. We only found out because our mothers ran into each other at the Star Market. After I had been there about a year, Jay Adler visited for about a week.

After the fourth runaround trip to the Electrical Board in an attempt to get my Journeyman's License, I applied for admission to the University of Colorado to become an electrical engineer. I started with adult education algebra. I worked my way through college. In my senior year, I switched from electrical engineering to mechanical engineering.

I had been a coop (intern) at the National Bureau of Standards and I took a job there when I graduated and have worked there ever since. I ran a cryogenic thermal conductivity program for several years. I also did research in superconductors, magnetic materials, welding, ultrasonics, x-ray diffraction, non-destructive testing, and a bunch of other things.

I'm just about to make a jump to the National Oceanographic and Atmospheric Administration to do research on pollutants in the upper atmosphere using laser ring-down spectroscopy. This should be fun.

I have some unusual hobbies.

I'm into electric vehicles in a big way. I hold the world record for an electric powered motorcycle in the 1/4 mile drag. (9.45 seconds at 152mph) <http://www.KillaCycle.com> I also drive an electric car. <http://www.haritech.com/wabbit.html> To top it all off, I've just started building an airplane in may garage. I'm just got married for the second time. I now have a total of four
stepchildren! I hope to make the reunion in September. See you there!
